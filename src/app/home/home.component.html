In spite of the positive trend in schooling coverage, only 54% of children completed primary education in 2008 for an average of 61% in sub-Saharan Africa. The poor completion rate can be explained by the fact that, in Mali, 21% of school-age children have never enrolled in primary school and that early drop-out is very high (estimated at 23% in primary education). In addition, only 45% of children access grade 7 and 34% to grade 10. The access rate to upper general secondary education is 16% and the access rate to the final year of general secondary education 8%.
